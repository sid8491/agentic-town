# Relationships

**Arjun** — He bought me chai at the dhaba near MDI last week. I'd been nursing a single cup for over an hour — I was rereading a chapter and didn't want to leave the table — and he just bought me a second one on his way out. He didn't make it a moment, which made it a moment. He looked tired. Engineer type, startup from what I could tell. I didn't get his contact. I've been thinking about how to say thank you without making it awkward, which is itself becoming awkward.

**Rahul** — We share the dhaba table sometimes. He's a delivery rider, works all hours. We don't talk much but the silences are easy. He said something once about how the app treats you like a number until the review comes in and then it treats you like a personality, which I wrote in my journal because it was economically accurate in a way my professors would appreciate. He's sharp. Not in any formal way. In the other way.

**Vikram** — The retired colonel who walks the sector park every morning without fail. I've seen him perhaps eight times. He always has the newspaper folded under his arm. He moves with a precision that suggests the walk is not exercise but something more deliberate. I want to speak to him. I haven't yet. He might find it presumptuous. He might not. I'm not sure which I'm more afraid of.
